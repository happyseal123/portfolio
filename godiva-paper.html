Godiva Rebranding Case Study

Context & Objective
  • For the final paper of an introductory business management course, I was tasked with reviewing the details of a Harvard Business School case, performing my own analysis, and ultimately coming up with a recommendation for the company at hand.
  • Because of my interest in consumer experiences, I chose to focus on a case published in 2015, "Rebranding Godiva: The Y覺ld覺z Strategy", which considered Godiva's brand positioning and potential entrance into mass market distribution channels such as super markets and pharmacies following the brand's acquisition by Y覺ld覺z Holding.
Critical Questions
  • In considering whether Godiva, a historically luxury brand, should enter the FDM (food, drugstore, and mass) channel, a few critical questions became apparent:
    • Can Godiva coexist succesfully in both the luxury and FDM channels?
    • How would shoppers differ between those buying from Godiva boutiques and those buying from FDM retailers?
    • What would the long-term effects of Godiva entering the FDM channel be?
    • What are alternative strategies to grow Godiva sales?
  • With these critical questions in mind, I could begin analysis.

    • Concentrate on growth of foreign sales in key markets such as Japan, China, and the Middle East
    • Invest in Godiva's North America brand image through increased advertising expenditure
    • Optimize the Godiva retail network through renovations, seasonal pop-ups, and closures of poorly performing stores

Takeaways
  • My work on this paper highlighted to me how important it is for consumer companies to have a long-term perspective on their brand image. While Godiva may have been able to temporarily increase revenues by entering the FDM channel, doing so would have ultimately diminished their brand image.
  • The SWOT framework proved to be a useful tool for analysis, and I am eager to become familiar with more complex business frameworks for strategic thinking.
