Hair Salon Website Redesign

Context & Objective
  • As a consumer myself, I am quick to judge product and service providers by the quality of their websites. Today more than ever, sites that function well and look attractive across the device spectrum (phone, tablet, computer) are crucial for an engaging consumer experience that ultimately yields business revenue.
  • Eager to gain experience crafting a well-designed responsive site from scratch, I decided to redesign the website for Moss Hair Salon, a full-service salon located in Providence, RI. The current Moss website looks like this:
Identifying Usability Issues
  • I started by careffuly analyzing the existing site to pinpoint usability issues that I could address in the redesign. Four main concerns became apparent:
    • Poor Learnability for New Users: It is unclear how to perform typical tasks, such as booking a hair appointment, and salon reviews are hidden within "Our Staff" page, which new users would not be aware of
    • Difficult to Determine State of the System: "Our Staff" page on navigation menu is highlighted even when user is on the home page, making it confusing to know what page one is currently on
    • Visual Design Problems: Low contrast areas make it difficult to read text, and unclear hierarchy on the site makes it challenging to find important information, such as the salon phone number
    • Acccessibility Concerns: In addition to low contrast areas, non-descriptive alt tags on images negatively impact the site's accessibility
